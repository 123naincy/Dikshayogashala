Syllabus of 300-Hour Yoga Teacher Training Course at Dikhshayogashala School

Dikhsayogashala’s 300-Hour Yoga Teacher Training course offers a transformative journey that deepens your practice, enhances your teaching skills, and provides you with a holistic understanding of yoga. Here is a detailed outline of the key subjects you will explore during the course:

Subtle Body Anatomy

Each day begins with Shatkarma, a practice of yogic detoxification that promotes internal cleansing. In Sanskrit, this is known as satkarma, and our expert instructors will guide you through the theory and application of each technique.

Shlokas (Sacred Verses)

Learn to recite and understand traditional Sanskrit Shlokas from the Yoga Sutras and other ancient texts. These sacred verses play a significant role in grounding your practice, enhancing concentration, and connecting to the rich spiritual heritage of yoga.

Restorative Yoga

Discover the techniques used in Restorative Yoga, which emphasizes deep relaxation and restorative postures to calm the nervous system and rejuvenate the body. You’ll learn how to teach this gentle practice to reduce stress and promote healing.

Pranayama (Breathing Techniques)

Gain expertise in various pranayama techniques, such as Ujjayi, Nadi Shodhana, and Kapalbhati, to control and regulate the breath. Pranayama enhances the flow of energy throughout the body, improves lung capacity, and calms the mind.

Advanced Methodology and Adjustments

Focus on advanced postural integration and learn the art of making precise adjustments for your students to ensure correct alignment, prevent injury, and deepen their practice. You’ll also study the nuances of verbal instruction to communicate effectively with students of different learning styles.

Chants, Mantras, and Shlokas

Engage in the recitation of mantras and Shlokas to enhance focus, memory, and meditation practice. Learn the significance of these sacred sounds in yoga, and how chanting can create a harmonious atmosphere in your classes.

Yoga Sutras of Patanjali

Dive into the teachings of Patanjali’s Yoga Sutras, which provide the philosophical foundation of yoga practice. Understand how these ancient principles guide your spiritual growth and personal development on and off the mat.

Anatomy & Physiology

Explore the internal systems of the human body, including the musculoskeletal, cardiovascular, and nervous systems, to understand how they interact with yoga practice. This knowledge will help you guide students safely and effectively in their physical practice.

Applied Anatomy

Learn how to apply anatomical knowledge to the practice of asanas. Understand how to identify and address common misalignments, helping students improve their posture, avoid injury, and deepen their practice.

Asana Practice

Master the fundamental and advanced yoga postures (asanas) with a focus on alignment, breath, and body awareness. This module will strengthen your personal practice and teach you how to guide your students through safe and effective sequences.

Sanskrit Terminology

Study the traditional Sanskrit names of asanas and yogic terms. This will deepen your connection to the ancient roots of yoga and help you teach with greater authenticity.

Yoga Philosophy

Understand the timeless teachings of yoga philosophy from ancient scriptures, including the Bhagavad Gita, the Upanishads, and the Yoga Sutras. Learn how to apply these teachings in your personal practice and adapt them for modern life.

Use of Props

Learn how to incorporate props like blocks, straps, and blankets into your classes to assist in alignment, deepen stretches, and make poses more accessible. This knowledge is essential for teaching students with different body types and abilities.

Mudras and Bandhas

Study the importance of Mudras (hand gestures) and Bandhas (body locks) in yoga. These techniques help regulate energy flow, enhance concentration, and deepen meditation. You will learn how to use these tools to refine your practice and teach your students more advanced practices.

Ayurvedic Diet and Nutrition

Explore the principles of Ayurveda and how diet and nutrition play a significant role in maintaining balance and supporting a yoga practice. Learn how to recommend food and lifestyle choices based on Ayurvedic doshas (body types) to enhance physical health and mental clarity.

Yoga Therapy

Understand the therapeutic applications of yoga in promoting physical and mental health. This module will cover how specific asanas, pranayama, and meditation techniques can support healing for common ailments, injuries, and chronic conditions.

Shatkarma (Cleansing Practices)

Learn the six traditional purification techniques of Shatkarma to detoxify the body and mind. These practices help clear blockages, improve digestion, and create balance in the body, supporting a deeper and more transformative yoga practice.

Suggested Study Material for the 300-Hour Yoga Teacher Training at Dikhsayogashala

As part of your transformative journey in the 300-Hour Yoga Teacher Training at Dikhsayogashala, we provide a carefully curated collection of texts that will deepen your understanding of yoga in its many dimensions.

These materials, written by renowned yogic masters, cover the essential aspects of yoga philosophy, anatomy, breathwork, asanas, and spiritual practices. Below is a list of key texts that will guide your studies during the course:

Light on Yoga – B.K.S. Iyengar

Light on Yoga by the legendary B.K.S. Iyengar is a cornerstone text for yoga practitioners worldwide. In this book, Iyengar provides a comprehensive approach to the practice of asanas, pranayama, and meditation, with a special emphasis on alignment and the integration of breath and movement. Throughout the 300-hour course, this text will guide you in refining your personal practice, while offering valuable insights into how to teach with precision and mindfulness.

Light on Pranayama – B.K.S. Iyengar

In Light on Pranayama, B.K.S. Iyengar delves deeply into the practice of pranayama (breath control), an essential component of yoga. This text provides detailed instructions on various pranayama techniques and their physiological and energetic benefits. During the course, you will explore how pranayama can enhance physical health, mental clarity, and spiritual growth, and learn how to incorporate these practices into your teaching.

Asana, Mudra, Bandha, Pranayama – Swami Satyananda Saraswati

Swami Satyananda Saraswati’s Asana, Mudra, Bandha, Pranayama offers a thorough exploration of the foundational practices of yoga. This book covers essential postures (asanas), mudras (hand gestures), bandhas (body locks), and pranayama techniques that are central to yoga’s physical and spiritual aspects. As part of the 300-hour course, you will learn how to incorporate these practices into your sessions to help students achieve balance, vitality, and inner peace.

The Yoga Sutras of Patanjali – Patanjali

The Yoga Sutras of Patanjali are the philosophical foundation of yoga practice. These 196 sutras outline the path to spiritual awakening, focusing on discipline, meditation, and ethical living. Patanjali’s teachings provide the framework for self-awareness and self-transformation. During the course, you will study these teachings in-depth, learning how to integrate them into your practice and teaching for personal growth and for guiding your students toward holistic well-being.

Hatha Yoga Pradipika – Swatmarama

Written by the sage Swatmarama, the Hatha Yoga Pradipika is one of the most important texts in the Hatha Yoga tradition. It outlines the essential practices of Hatha Yoga, including asanas, pranayama, and purification techniques (Shatkarma). This text will deepen your understanding of the body’s role in spiritual practice and provide practical guidance for teaching these techniques to others. Swatmarama’s teachings emphasize the balance of physical strength, flexibility, and mental clarity—important principles you’ll explore throughout your training.

Moola Bandha – Swami Buddhananda

Moola Bandha by Swami Buddhananda is an essential resource for understanding one of yoga’s most powerful practices—the root lock. Moola Bandha helps regulate the flow of prana (life energy) within the body, focusing on the pelvic floor to stimulate vitality and energy. In your 300-hour training, you will learn the technique in detail, including how to incorporate it into your asana practice and use it for energy control and spiritual awakening.

Prana and Pranayama – Swami Niranjananda Saraswati

Prana and Pranayama by Swami Niranjananda Saraswati offers a deep dive into the science of breath and prana (life energy). This text explains the techniques of pranayama in detail, exploring the intricate relationship between breath and energy flow in the body. Through this study, you will gain a comprehensive understanding of how pranayama can be used to balance and harmonize the body, mind, and spirit. Swami Niranjananda’s teachings will be crucial in your development as a teacher, helping you guide students in their breathwork and meditation practices.
