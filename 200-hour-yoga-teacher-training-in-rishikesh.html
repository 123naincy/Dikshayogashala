Syllabus of 200-Hour Yoga Teacher Training Course at Dikhshayogashala School

At Dikhshayogashala School, our comprehensive 200-hour Yoga Teacher Training Course (TTC) is designed to deepen your understanding of yoga and equip you with the skills needed to teach effectively. Below is an overview of our syllabus, highlighting the key components of your training.

Shatkarma: Yogic Detoxification

Each day begins with Shatkarma, a practice of yogic detoxification that promotes internal cleansing. In Sanskrit, this is known as satkarma, and our expert instructors will guide you through the theory and application of each technique.

Key Practices Include:

  • Jal Neti:A nasal cleansing technique using warm saline water and a neti pot, provided by Dikhshayogashala School. This practice helps clear nasal passages and improves breathing by removing any blockages.
  • Sutraneti: Another nasal cleansing method utilizing a cotton thread for advanced practitioners and a rubber tube for beginners, designed to enhance respiratory health.
  • Trataka: This eye-cleaning practice involves gazing at a candle flame to improve concentration and clarity of vision.
  • Kapalbhati: Known as skull shining breath, this pranayama technique aids in detoxifying the lungs and boosting energy levels while enhancing respiratory function.
  • Kunjal (Vaman): A powerful abdominal cleansing practice that leaves you feeling rejuvenated and light, promoting overall health and vitality.

Yoga Alignment

Understanding alignment is crucial for both personal practice and effective teaching. In this section, we will explore:

  • The importance of alignment in yoga and its impact on physical health.
  • How to address conditions like flat feet, high arches, knock knees, and bow legs through targeted yoga practices.
  • Techniques for improving posture in daily life and practice.
  • The principles of perfect alignment in standing, sitting, and sleeping asanas.
  • The alignment of sun salutations in Hatha yoga, including the foundational poses necessary for effective practice.
  • How to utilize yoga props to enhance your asana practice.
  • Detailed alignment principles for the primary series, including the finishing sequence, to prepare you for teaching these essential practices.
  • Strategies to enhance your teaching skills for more effective instruction.

Suggested Reading Materials for 200-Hour Yoga Teacher Training at Dikhsayogashala

To support your learning and deepen your understanding of yoga during the 200-Hour Yoga Teacher Training at Dikhsayogashala, we recommend the following texts. These readings cover essential aspects of yoga philosophy, anatomy, asana, and pranayama, providing a solid foundation for both personal practice and teaching.

Yoga Philosophy: Four Chapters of Freedom – Bihar School of Yoga

This book is an essential guide to understanding the spiritual teachings of yoga. Four Chapters of Freedom by the Bihar School of Yoga delves into the philosophy of yoga as laid out by ancient sages. It explores the four chapters of the Yoga Sutras of Patanjali, offering practical interpretations of the texts that focus on the path to liberation through yoga. This book will provide you with valuable insights into yoga’s ethical and spiritual principles, essential for a well-rounded practice.

Yoga Anatomy: The Key Muscles of Hatha Yoga – Ray Long

This text offers a detailed anatomical perspective on the key muscles involved in Hatha Yoga postures. Yoga Anatomy by Ray Long breaks down the muscles engaged in common asanas, making it easier to understand their alignment and function. By studying this book, you will gain a deeper understanding of the body’s biomechanics, which is crucial for improving your practice and teaching students with varying body types and abilities.

Asana, Pranayama, Mudra, Bandha – Swami Satyananda Saraswati

In this classic work, Swami Satyananda Saraswati provides an in-depth study of the physical practices of yoga. Asana, Pranayama, Mudra, Bandha covers the key aspects of asana (postures), pranayama (breath control), mudras (gestures), and bandhas (body locks), offering clear instructions on how to perform and teach these techniques. This book is a must-read for anyone wishing to understand the connection between breath, body, and mind in yoga practice.

Light on Pranayama – B.K.S. Iyengar

Light on Pranayama by B.K.S. Iyengar is a comprehensive guide to the practice of pranayama, or breath control. This book explores the significance of pranayama in yoga, offering detailed explanations of various techniques and their effects on the body and mind. By studying this book, you will gain a deeper understanding of the breath’s role in calming the mind, enhancing concentration, and supporting overall well-being.
