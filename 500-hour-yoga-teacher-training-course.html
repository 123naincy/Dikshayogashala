Syllabus of 500-Hour Yoga Teacher Training Course at Dikhshayogashala School

Dikhsayogashala’s 500-Hour Yoga Teacher Training course offers a transformative journey that deepens your practice, enhances your teaching skills, and provides you with a holistic understanding of yoga. Here is a detailed outline of the key subjects you will explore during the course:

Shatkarma: Yogic Detoxification

The day begins with Shatkarma, or Satkarma, which refers to the ancient yogic practice of internal body cleansing. This vital aspect of yoga focuses on detoxifying the body to promote health, vitality, and mental clarity. Our highly experienced teachers will guide you through each Shatkarma technique, explaining the theory, the benefits, and the proper methods for performing them.

Our teachers simplify Shatkarma, making it accessible and effective for every student.

Techniques of Shatkarma:

  • Jal Neti (Nasal Cleansing): This technique involves using salty, warm water and a Neti pot (provided by the school) to clear the nasal passages.
  • Benefits: Helps improve breathing by removing blockages in the nose, enhancing respiratory health.
  • Sutraneti (Advanced Nasal Cleansing): For advanced practitioners, this process involves using a cotton thread or, for beginners, a rubber tube to cleanse the nasal passages.
  • Benefits: Clears the sinuses, improves focus, and supports overall respiratory health.
  • Trataka (Eye Cleansing): A powerful technique for cleansing and strengthening the eyes using two types of methods
  • Benefits: Improves concentration, relieves eye strain, and enhances vision
  • Kapalbhati (Skull Cleansing): A form of pranayama that focuses on skull cleansing. It involves rapid exhalation through the nose, strengthening the respiratory system and cleansing the mind.
  • BenefitsImproves respiratory health, increases vitality, helps with weight management, and enhances mental clarity.
  • Kunjal (Abdominal Cleansing):○ Known as Vaman, Kunjal involves the practice of abdominal cleansing by inducing vomiting. It is one of the most effective detoxifying techniques.
  • BenefitsRemoves toxins from the digestive system, leaving you feeling light and healthy.

Vinyasa Flow

What is Vinyasa Flow?

Vinyasa Flow is a dynamic style of yoga where breath and movement are synchronized into a flowing sequence of postures. In this segment, we explore the essence of Vinyasa Flow and how it differs from traditional Vinyasa.

Topics Covered in Vinyasa Flow:
  1. Introduction to Vinyasa Flow:
    • The history and evolution of Vinyasa Flow.
    • How it differs from other styles of yoga
  2. How to Teach Vinyasa Flow:
    • Why teaching Vinyasa Flow can be challenging and how to overcome these difficulties.
    • Developing the ability to create flowing sequences that match the students' level and needs.
  3. Designing Sequences:
    • Learn how to create well-structured sequences for Vinyasa Flow classes, taking into account various aspects like duration, difficulty, and energy levels.
    • Guidelines for designing Vinyasa Flow sequences to ensure proper flow and alignment.
  4. Identifying and Correcting Common Mistakes:
    • Recognizing and correcting bad habits in asanas to ensure the safety and effectiveness of the practice.
    • Focus on alignment and proper breathwork to support movement.

Yoga Alignment

What is Alignment and Why is it Important?

Alignment is the foundation of a safe and effective yoga practice. Proper alignment helps prevent injury, improves posture, and enhances the benefits of each pose. In this module, we will explore how to achieve optimal alignment in various poses and common challenges that practitioners face.

Topics Covered in Yoga Alignment:

  1. Understanding Foot Alignment:
    • Flat feet and high arches: Learn how yoga can help balance and correct foot posture.
    • Cure methods for imbalances through specific poses.
  2. Knee Alignment:
    • Knock knees and bow legs: Discover how yoga can help correct these conditions, improving balance and joint health.
  3. Alignment in Sun Salutations:
    • Proper alignment techniques in Hatha Yoga and Ashtanga Vinyasa Sun Salutations to build strength and flexibility.
  4. Alignment in Ashtanga Primary Series:
    • Detailed focus on the alignment of each posture in the Ashtanga Primary Series, including the finishing sequence.
    • Learn to teach alignment cues for various levels of students.
  5. Using Props to Enhance Asana Practice:
    • Learn how to use yoga props such as blocks, straps, and bolsters to deepen alignment, increase comfort, and prevent injury
  6. Perfecting the Posture:
    • Achieving perfect alignment in poses, whether standing, sitting, or lying down.
    • Learn how alignment improves the overall effectiveness of your practice.

Improving Your Teaching Skills

In this segment, we will focus on improving your teaching abilities, especially in the context of yoga alignment and sequencing.

  1. Refining Your Instruction
    • How to deliver clear and effective teaching cues to guide students into proper alignment and movement.
    • Techniques for improving communication and creating a positive, supportive teaching environment
  2. Building Confidence in Teaching
    • Learn how to teach from your own experience, and how to guide more experienced practitioners based on their prior knowledge.
    • Encourage students to refine their practice while respecting their individual limits.
